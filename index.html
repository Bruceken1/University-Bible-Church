Who We Are

University Bible Church is an independent Evangelical Bible Teaching Christian church in the Protestant tradition, located in Westwood, Los Angeles. Founded to serve Bible-believing residents and UCLA students, we emphasize expository preaching and gracious hospitality.

Our History

Founded in the 1920s-1930s (property since 1928, organized 1931 originally as Presbyterian, later undenominational Bible Church). For nearly a century at Wilshire & Malcolm, once the tallest building in Westwood. Committed to uniting believers through the teaching of God's Word.

"Combining the warmth of a gracious Christian hospitality with the fervor of a personal, devotional application of the great doctrines and eternal truths of God's Word..." — Milo F. Jamison, Founder

Our Mission

To glorify God through Christ-honoring, Bible-teaching ministry, maintaining balance — avoiding fanatical cultism or modernistic neo-orthodoxy. We focus on the exposition of Scripture as the power for salvation and Christian living.

"The great need across evangelicalism is exposition of the Scriptures." — J Dwight Pentecost

Leadership

Senior Pastor: Bryan Moselle (third-generation Angeleno, serving since 2016). We value lay service and the call for Christians to serve in every sphere of life.

"Christians are called to God's service not only in church professions but also in every secular calling." — J Gresham Machen

Articles of Faith

We hold to the historic Christian faith, including the inerrancy of Scripture, the Trinity, the deity and saving work of Jesus Christ, salvation by grace through faith, and a premillennial eschatology.

Core Beliefs

  • The Bible is the inspired, inerrant Word of God in its original manuscripts.
  • The Trinity: One God in three Persons — Father, Son, Holy Spirit.
  • Jesus Christ: Fully God and fully man; His virgin birth, sinless life, atoning death, bodily resurrection, ascension, and literal return.
  • Salvation by grace through personal faith in Jesus Christ alone.

Eschatology

We teach premillennialism: Christ will return literally to establish His kingdom on earth, fulfilling Israel's covenants, reigning for 1,000 years before transferring the kingdom to the Father (Rev 20–21; 1 Cor 15:24-25).

"We must meet the present situation, and we must speak a word to the world that no one else can speak." — D Martyn Lloyd-Jones
